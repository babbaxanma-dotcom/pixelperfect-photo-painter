Architectural exterior photograph of a Belgian Flemish karaktervilla (1960s era detached home) with a newly restored natural slate roof, photographed from the front-corner. The dak is a hellend zadeldak fully covered in Spanish natuurleien Cupa Heavy 3 — dark blue-grey natural slate, neat overlapping rows clearly visible across the full dakvlak. Original brick chimneys preserved on the ridge. Below the dak: a beige-cream painted plaster gevel with white-painted window frames and decorative ornamental detailing typical of 1960s Flemish karakterwoningen — slightly arched windows, modest stoeppartij. Restored handgeklopte koperen kielgoten visible at the dakranden — warm copper colour against the slate. Mature trees in the established voortuin (cypress, magnolia), tegelpad to the inkomdeur, low boxwood hedge along the gevel. Soft overcast Belgian daylight — even diffuse lighting that shows the slate texture without harsh shadows. Shot on Sony A7R IV, 35mm lens, f/11, ISO 200, perfectly vertical lines, ultra-sharp focus from voortuin to nokpan. Professional architectural photography editorial style, no people, no cars.
